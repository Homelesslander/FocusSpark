Different Neurodevelopmental Profiles

Overview

Many children are described using different diagnostic labels. These labels are helpful for accessing support and understanding how their neurodiverse brain works however it should not limit or define the bounds of any child's potential.

Autism

Common Support Strategies for Autism include:

  • May benefit from predictable, clear routines.
  • Responds well to visual supports.
  • Sensory sensitivities may affect behaviour.
  • Predictable schedules can help transitions between environments.

ADHD

Persons with ADHD may have difficulty paying attention, planning and completing tasks. Some strategies to assist such persons include:

  • Development of structured routines and short, clear instructions.
  • Positive reinforcement to support motivation.
  • Structured breaks in between activities.

Specific Learning Differences (Dyslexia, Dyscalculia, Dysgraphia)

Dyslexia, dyscalculia, and dysgraphia are common, lifelong neurodevelopmental learning disorders affecting reading, math, and writing skills, respectively. These types of persons often require:

  • Multisensory teaching methods.
  • More time to complete academic tasks.
  • Visual supports and step-by-step breakdowns.

Anxiety-Related Differences

Anxiety conditions can cause persons to feel fear and tension in situations of extreme stress. These types of persons supported through:

  • Creation of Predictable Schedules Help Reduce Worry.
  • Mindfulness/calm down breaks.
  • Reduced Uncertainty of Task Outcomes.
